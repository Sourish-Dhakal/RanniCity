The Ranni City

Fixing the overpopulated land of Kerala, India.

Problems we have identified in Kerala / India.

Overpopulation

We're in India, and India has a very high population density. One of our problems to solve is overpopulation and having enough places for all the people who live in the area. The Ranni City needs to be more dense with apartments and houses to fit all the people that live here.

Climate Change

Kerala is already experiencing the effects of climate change, including increased flooding and other extreme weather events. These impacts could make it even more challenging to create a sustainable city, as the infrastructure and systems put in place would need to be resilient to these impacts.

Cost

India is a country with lots of people. Having more people means there needs to be more jobs. If there aren't enough jobs to fit all the people, then the economy would be ruined. It's important that we get the people jobs and we've made sure they will always have a place to work at.

Cultural Resistance

Kerala has a strong cultural heritage and tradition, and there may be resistance to change from some residents who are attached to their way of life. For example, some people may be resistant to changes in transportation patterns or building design.

City Features / Need Statement

Tree Planting Policy

The policy will ensure that at least one tree will be present on all properties owned by an individual, business or the government. However, the number of trees will vary on each property depending on the size of the land's area. Our ambition is for communities to become carbon dioxide-free, allowing future generations to breathe in fresh, purified air instead of emissions from factories that overpower the region.

Rooftop Gardens

Keeping the rooftop gardens functional and sustainable for a long time, residents are required to regularly water, harvest, and sell crops back to the community. Guiding the residents of the city by having further accessibility to locally grown, fresh food, which will not only increase the air quality in the area from fewer cars being on the road heading to grocery stores but furthermore provide the people with food that is not chemically processed, abused and unhealthy.

Plastic Ban

Over the past decades, the use of plastic items and other forms of litter/waste has increased drastically, which lead to higher waste production at an alarming rate, causing more factors to increase in numbers that will affect Earth’s ecosystem, such as higher animal deaths, more polluted landscapes, oceans, skies and more. Due to this, we have strictly decided to ban all use of plastics within our future settlement, as it has been done before in other foreign countries, such as Rwanda, Kenya, Bangladesh and St. Kitts and Nevis.

Reducing The Dependency of Fossil Fuels

To keep our settlement sustainable for many generations to come, the residents would need to rely on a renewable source of energy that will provide them with daily life essentials like electricity, water, and heat for a long time. Therefore, we have decided to reduce our settlement's dependency on fossil fuels, like oil and gas, and move forward to using renewable sources from the environment.
